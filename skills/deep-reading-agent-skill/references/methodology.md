# Deep Reading Methodology: Nuance, Concept, & Spirit

To perform a "Deep Reading," you must look beyond the surface level of the text. This document defines the three dimensions of reflection required for this skill.

## 1. Nuance (The "How")
Nuance is the subtle distinction or variation in meaning, expression, or tone. It is found in what is *not* explicitly stated but strongly implied.

*   **Look for:**
    *   **Implicit Assumptions:** What must be true for this statement to make sense?
    *   **Tone & Intent:** Is the author being cautious, assertive, skeptical, or visionary?
    *   **Comparative Distinctions:** How does this specific phrasing differ from a more generic way of saying the same thing?
*   **Example:** If a technical guide says "Ideally, use X," the nuance is that X is the gold standard, but there are likely real-world constraints that often make X impossible or impractical.

## 2. Concept (The "What")
A concept is an abstract idea or a fundamental building block of the system or philosophy being described.

*   **Look for:**
    *   **Definition & Boundaries:** What exactly is this idea, and where does it end?
    *   **Relationships:** How does this concept interact with other concepts in the text?
    *   **Novelty:** Is this a new way of thinking about an old problem?
*   **Example:** The concept of "Progressive Disclosure" is not just about hiding information; it's about managing cognitive load and ensuring the agent's attention is focused on the most relevant data at any given time.

## 3. Spirit (The "Why")
The spirit is the overarching intent, the "soul," or the core philosophy of the work. It is the destination the author wants the reader to arrive at.

*   **Look for:**
    *   **The Ultimate Goal:** Why was this resource written? What world-view is it trying to promote?
    *   **Cohesion:** How do all the individual nuances and concepts align to form a single, unified message?
    *   **The "Vibe":** What is the feeling or energy behind the text? (e.g., pragmatic, radical, or conservative).
*   **Example:** The spirit of the "Agent Skills" specification is not just about folder structures; it's about creating a portable, standard language that allows diverse AI agents to understand and execute complex workflows across different environments.

## Reflection Checklist
When reflecting on a section, ask yourself:
1.  What is the author *trying* to say that they didn't explicitly write down? (Nuance)
2.  If I had to explain this idea to a child using only one word, what would it be? (Concept)
3.  If this section was a person, what would their personality be? (Spirit)
